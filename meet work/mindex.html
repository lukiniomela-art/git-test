The Tbilisi Zoo is the oldest and largest zoo in Georgia, founded in 1927. Animals: The zoo features a wide variety of animals, including lions, tigers, elephants, and reptiles, as well as endemic species from the Caucasus region.

our animals

bear

In zoos, bears are a common attraction, with species like grizzlies, polar bears, and sun bears housed in enclosures. While some modern zoos design habitats to maximize animal welfare, housing bears in captivity is ethically complex and remains a subject of controversy.

wolf

Keeping wolves in a zoo environment involves careful consideration of their social, physical, and psychological needs. Exhibiting these highly social and wide-ranging animals raises complex issues about animal welfare, conservation, and the ethics of captivity.

lion

Lions in zoos, unlike their wild counterparts, have a significantly longer lifespan due to consistent veterinary care, protection from predators, and a steady supply of food. However, their daily life is dramatically different, including their diet, social structure, and activity levels.
